### 1b. Working Philosophy

Moore works by a principle he calls *sprezzatura* — the Italian Renaissance concept of studied carelessness, effortlessness achieved through invisible difficulty. "I try very hard to make things look effortless, but I'm often drawn to the complicated path from an interior design perspective. I relish in the details and the craftsmanship of things that are not easily reproduced." [Aspire Metro, December 2022]

That effort is in service of a specific conviction: rooms are built from the owner's life, not the designer's taste. "My projects share a traditional sensibility enhanced by uncommon details curated from the owner's Object Biography." [Aspire Metro, December 2022] The objects, palette, and materials in a Doniphan Moore room are pulled from how a specific person inhabits the world — "intentional artifacts from a well-lived life." [D'KOR Home, 2020]

The Kips Bay Dallas 2025 River Room demonstrates both principles at work: Max Xeno Karnig's *Le Tricheur* hangs above a Jules Leleu cabinet made for the steamship *L'Atlantique*. Caravaggio's *Cardsharps*, reread for a room designed for exactly that kind of game. The original is at the Kimbell — twenty minutes away. Every object traces to a decision, every decision to the room's purpose. [Style Guide, April 2026]

The capturing phrase is *sprezzatura* itself — a word Moore has publicly claimed as his working method, and which no other major American designer in his market is using. [Aspire Metro, December 2022]

What keeps him in this work: the relish of difficulty. The complicated path. "I relish in the details and the craftsmanship of things that are not easily reproduced." The work is not a burden to be hidden — it's the thing that sustains him. The rooms absorb the labor; the feed absorbs the standard.

### 2c. Voice Register

#### Voice Profile

Doniphan Moore's voice is singular — one principal, one standard. There is no blended voice or team register. The voice runs from blunt and declarative (the default for project content) to dry and observational (cultural content, event coverage, longer-form writing). Both modes share the same discipline: compression, material specificity, and refusal to explain.

The default register — project content, room reveals, object posts — is the shortest and most controlled. One strong noun carries more weight than three adjectives. The cultural register — art fairs, show house panels, the quarterly *IV* — extends to 2-3 paragraphs with the feel of an entry from a well-read person's notebook.

- **Length:** 1–3 sentences for project content. If a fourth appears, delete the weakest one first. Cultural posts (art fairs, show houses) extend to 2–3 paragraphs. [Style Guide, April 2026]
- **Material specificity over mood language:** Every quality word earns its place only when paired with a named material, object, or place. Burnished pewter. Calacatta Viola. Lance woven leather. De Gournay custom colorway. [Style Guide, April 2026]
- **Fashion language as native vocabulary:** "Well-dressed," "silhouette," "cut," "masculine and feminine" applied to rooms are authorized. Not metaphorical — compositional. [doniphanmoore.com; Aspire Metro, December 2022]
- **Art references deployed, not paraphrased:** Klimt, Caravaggio, garment construction — cite without explanation. "Art-inspired" is a paraphrase; the reference itself is the content. [Style Guide, April 2026; Aspire Metro, December 2022]
- **Seasonal references are sensory, not calendar:** "Verdigris" over "spring palette." Never anticipate the next season. "Be happy where you are." [Style Guide, April 2026]

## Section 3: The Content Card

### 3a. Operational Standards

- **Photographer credit is mandatory on every post.** Douglas Friedman → "Photo by Douglas Friedman" or @douglasfriedman. Beckley Co → "Photographer: @beckleyco." Ori Harpaz, Nathan Schroder — same format. No exceptions.
- **Music on video content must be selected before export.** "I get viscerally agitated every fucking time music comes on and I immediately mute it." [Style Guide, April 2026] Auto-assigned or platform-suggested music is not acceptable.
- **No content that chases reach at the expense of feel.** "I don't want to be running after it so specifically that we end up losing the quality or the feeling of things." [Style Guide, April 2026] Content that does not meet Moore's standard for sophistication is not a gap-filler — it is a liability.
- **No fake materials or imprecise architecture in imagery.** "Fake marble (i.e. quartz that tries to be marble)" is a design pet peeve with professional consequences. [Aspire Metro, December 2022]
- **Pronoun policy is nuanced.** Most approved captions carry no pronoun at all. Default to pronoun-free; "I" in personal reflection; "we" only in collective/philosophical framing.

### Visual Standards

- Alternate close detail shots with wider architectural shots in the grid. Avoid two corner-angled frames in succession. Vary the directionality of images. "As an interior designer, I should know how to compose a feed. Right now it doesn't make me want to scroll." [Transcript, 2026-04-02]
- Lead image must work without the caption — the caption deepens, not rescues.
- Saturday posts have outperformed weekday content. Sunday is being tested. Treat Saturday as the primary cadence anchor — the week's strongest image belongs here. [Transcript, 2026-04-02]

### 3b. Content Tests — Five Gates

| Test | The Question | What It Catches |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| The Swap Test | Could you replace Doniphan Moore's name with a competitor's and have the caption still work? If yes, rewrite it. | If the name is interchangeable, the brand is invisible. Run on every single post. |
| The Voice Test | Does this sound like Doniphan Moore, or like someone describing Doniphan Moore? Read it aloud. | Authentic captured voice vs. manufactured approximation. |
| The "So What" Test | Does this caption say why this matters — not just what it looks like? | Description is the lowest form of caption. A point of view is what the audience saves. |
| The Feeling Test | Does someone scrolling past feel something before they read the caption? Does the caption match that feeling? | Image and caption should be in conversation, not competition. |
| The Way-of-Living Test | Does the caption (a) trace the choice to this specific owner's life, AND (b) name the material, vendor, stone, or decision specifically? Both must pass. | Category words ("jewel tones," "warm metallic") fail. "Amethyst," "burnished pewter," "Lance woven leather" pass. The named detail is the proof of biographical sourcing. |
| The Sophistication Test | Would Doniphan look at this and say "not sophisticated enough"? [Transcript, 2026-04-02] | Not "is this elegant" — but "does this belong in a feed watched by people actively evaluating him for projects." |

### Pillar Briefs

**Object Biography**
Belief: Every object in a room was made necessary by the specific person who lives in it. Objects are "intentional artifacts from a well-lived life" — biography, not inventory. [D'KOR Home, 2020]
Categories: First-install reveals (full room and vignette), object provenance, vendor and artist credit posts, show house room reveals (Kips Bay, Texas Design Week), single hero details that carry the room.
Caption pattern: Opens with a named material or object — no warm-up, no adjective. Adds one piece of specific detail — provenance, fabrication, source, decision context. Names the makers if visible. Closes with a single editorial observation (optional).
Format and frequency: Single image or carousel. Minimum 2 posts per week.
Example: "The game room at Surf's Edge. De Gournay custom Fishes — a colorway developed specifically for this room — floor to ceiling. The chair is Lance woven leather; the stripe was custom-woven to spec. The lamp: leather, brass, antique copper, thick glass base. Amethyst. Mauve. **Sprezzatura**
Belief: "I try very hard to make things look effortless, but I'm often drawn to the complicated path." [Aspire Metro, December 2022] The resolution he applies in a room does not lower for the feed. "There's hours of calibration before a room is finished." [Style Guide, April 2026]
Categories: Process video and carousels (calibration sequences, finish trials), finish-detail close-ups (the woven stripe, the patina, the seam), in-progress documentation (with client permission), studio moments (drawings, samples, decision-making artifacts).

**Lingering**
Belief: "True luxury exists in enjoying and lingering in one's personal space." [Aspire Metro, December 2022] "Rooms should live, breathe, and evolve over time." [D Magazine, Spring 2024] Rooms are designed to be stayed in, not photographed.
Categories: Year-X revisits (a library at year three, a kitchen at year five), rooms in use (a table set, a fire lit, a corner with a book left open), seasonal references to inhabited rooms, long-form project follow-ups (what the room has become), the "lived" detail shot (a worn arm, a hand on a glass, the patina that took five years to earn).
Caption pattern: Lower volume of words. The image carries weight; the caption is observational. Often pronoun-free. Seasonal references are sensory, never calendar — "verdigris" over "spring palette." Never anticipates the next season.
Format and frequency: Single image primary; short video occasionally. 1 post per week minimum. The pillar is the natural home for the lowest-pressure, highest-confidence posts in the rotation.
Examples: "Westway, Highland Park. Year five. The chair has earned its place. The leather has lived." / "Spring arrives in verdigris." / "I never get tired of khaki and cream."
